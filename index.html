Reddit Collectible Avatars

🎨
$75k+ revenue 5,900+ avatars sold 2,400+ unique collectors 1.5M+ GIPHY impressions

Overview

End-to-end product ownership in Reddit's Creator Program

Reddit users had limited ways to express identity and customize their presence on the platform. Reddit invited me to participate in a curated release of blockchain-backed collectible digital avatars — sold directly to consumers on the platform.

As a relatively experimental program, many specifics were left to individual creators to drive. I owned the full product lifecycle: user research, design, cross-functional delivery with Reddit eng and art direction, launch, iteration, and IP expansion — navigating rapidly changing program conditions to drive consistent impact.

🔍

Discovery

Five user research insights that drove design decisions

Before each release I analyzed prior-gen sales data, conducted direct interviews with collectors, and engaged in ongoing artist community discussions. Key findings that shaped product decisions:

  • Customizability wins: Color-changing and neutral (black-and-white) designs sold best — users wanted avatars they could mix across different sets. Layered transparency effects revealing hidden art when mashed up were especially valued, despite requiring more design effort.
  • Visual impact at small scale: Bright gold, large traits, and designs that "pop" at thumbnail size in storefront and trading platform views consistently outperformed more intricate designs that lost detail when small.
  • Crop-awareness matters: Avatars render differently across devices and are most often seen as a cropped head circle in comments. Designs optimizing head-space over full-body were more impactful day-to-day.
  • Scarcity vs. accessibility tension: Collector voice chats surfaced a real split — power collectors wanted limited high-price drops, but the overall collector base grew because of accessible, lower-cost options. Both product types needed to coexist.
  • Artist engagement is a product feature: Collectors wanted to follow the journey, not just own the output. Community engagement and visibility drove sales beyond the design itself.
⚙️

Build & Delivery

12 avatars shipped across multiple generations

Each avatar: 7–9 fully layered SVG/PNG files (hat, head, hair, body, lower body, accessories, background) built to Reddit's engineering template requirements. Delivered across multiple generational releases on schedule.

  • Applied brand guidelines and technical hex-code/SVG specs in every asset to ensure platform compatibility
  • Coordinated with Reddit's art director and engineering team on new features and release timing
  • Selected release price points and quantities based on collector demand signals, tuning with each drop
  • Created promo reels in Adobe Premiere for pre-release anticipation marketing — directly responding to user feedback that artist engagement drives collection activity

Mini case: "Spirit Seeker" accessory — applied all five design principles: large scale, neutral color, visible in head-profile view, fantasy-themed. Became the second-highest selling single trait accessory in the shop, with 1,000+ copies sold.

🛍️

Product Influence Beyond Design

Storefront UX improvements and shop experience

User feedback consistently surfaced a pain point beyond just avatar design: the storefront itself felt cluttered and hard to navigate. Rather than waiting for engineering solutions, I proposed lightweight curation initiatives:

  • Artist-of-the-Week feature: Suggested curating the storefront around a single artist and style — became a real program feature. I was the first artist featured as a solo artist-of-the-week (May 2025).
  • Thematic display banner designs: Proposed and designed banner concepts to improve visual wayfinding in the shop — no additional engineering required, pure content and layout improvements.

This kind of low-lift product influence — identifying friction, proposing solutions within existing constraints, and seeing them adopted — is the work I find most satisfying as a product thinker.

⚔️

Part II — Avatar Arena (2025)

From profile picture to playable game character

Situation: Persistent user feedback requested new use cases for avatars beyond profile pictures. I explored more immersive gameplay within Reddit's Devvit development platform, minimizing engineering requirements.

Action: Designed and led development of a battle gameplay experience where users fight with their own Reddit avatars. Custom mash-up saves sync in real-time via the Reddit API — your avatar in the app reflects whatever you've set on-site.

  • Ran a soft launch prototype that attracted 100+ organic playtesters within 24 hours, eager to give feedback on improvements
  • Collected structured feedback during planning and testing phases to guide iteration
  • Awarded $1k honorable mention at the 2025 Reddit × Kiro AI Hackathon in the Consumer Play category — recognized for particularly user-friendly experience design

Mobile Product · Android · UX Research · Data Analysis

Shipped Mobile Games

🎮
30k+ downloads Google Play + Amazon 2 shipped titles 4 years of data analyzed

Overview

Full lifecycle product ownership from first prototype to 30k downloads

I led end-to-end development of two Android games — owning product vision, art direction, development coordination, launch, and post-launch iteration through data and user feedback. Both were shipped independently with no existing team structure or template.

Adopt-a-Blob (2014–2018) reached 30,000+ downloads. Ramen Clicker Collectible Shop (2018–2020) followed, applying direct lessons from structured user research on idle clicker communities.

🐙

Case 1 — Adopt-a-Blob (2014–2018)

Virtual pet + collecting + customization

Situation: Casual virtual pet games lacked meaningful personalization and long-term engagement paths beyond basic care loops.

Action: Built the game using rapid prototyping — designing all assets, UI/UX, wearables, and interactive elements. Launched on Google Play, then iterated on real user reviews. A key user-prompted addition was an entire suite of 3–4 minigames, introduced because reviews surfaced demand for more active gameplay alongside the slower collection loop.

Data: Conducted a Python analysis of four years of data — CTR, ad revenue vs. download rates, user retention, and store visibility trends — to inform localization strategy and future store optimization.

Result: 30,000+ downloads across Google Play and Amazon App Store.

🍜

Case 2 — Ramen Clicker (2018–2020)

Cozy idle clicker built from user research up

Situation: Idle clickers drove engagement through numerical progress but typically lacked strong visual aesthetic or personalization. An opportunity to serve players who wanted both.

Action: Before building, conducted user interviews on Reddit idle clicker subreddits to gather real feedback on what players wanted. Built in Unity 2D with GitHub version control, delivering all game assets — animated background/foreground elements, UI/HUD, progress meters, and interactive elements.

Post-launch, user feedback specifically identified friction in ad integration and navigation flows. These were revised in subsequent releases — ad transitions and screen flow were areas difficult to test pre-release but quickly flagged by real players.

Result: Iterative releases received progressively higher ratings than initial launch, with positive player feedback on UX improvements.
